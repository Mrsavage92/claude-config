# Measured Reference Corpus (Zack D. Films, top 10 by views, analyzed 2026-07-13)

Ten videos, 8.6M-19M views each, torn down with scene detection, loudness metering, and word-level transcription. These are measurements, not opinions. When another rule in this skill contradicts this file, this file wins.

**Read this file as retention PHYSICS, not a style to copy.** Everything here is a universal attention mechanic (premise-drop grammar, causality chains, hot audio, loop-back endings) that is safe and correct to adopt. It is NOT a licence to reproduce the reference creator's visual look, voice, or topic list - that is his signature identity and copying it fails the clone-perception gate. See differentiation.md: adopt the physics, wear your own skin.

## Format distribution (what actually gets 10M+ views)

3x animal behaviour told as drama | 3x second-person hypothetical ("Imagine you...", "If you dropped a megalodon...") | 1x absurd how-to | 1x anonymized true-crime mystery | 1x dark real-world rule | 1x AI stunt. The unifying shape: **one irresistible premise, tested to its conclusion.** Not explanation - consequence.

## The language system

**Cold opens are premise-drops, never questions to camera.** All ten first lines drop into mid-action or a conditional premise:
- "When the fish lays an egg, a catfish will come to eat it and replace it with her own egg."
- "Imagine you wake up and everyone on your flight is unconscious."
- "The glass is placed between the pike and the fish, the pike lunges, and slams straight into it."
- "If you dropped a megalodon into an aquarium tank, its 80-foot body would crush the tank walls."
- "If someone falls off the safari ride, the driver won't stop, they'll speed up."
- "They left their children sleeping in the hotel to go to a restaurant just a few hundred feet away."
- "The beetle is impaled on the thorn before its head is torn off by the bird."

The audience's question ("wait, why?") is created by the premise, never spoken by the narrator.

**Chained causality carries the middle.** Sentences link by consequence: "And since... / Then... / But... / So... / until... / which is why...". Two signature devices:
- Sentence-initial **"Now,"** as a mid-video re-engagement pivot (5 of 10 videos).
- **"Meaning..."** to open the payoff/reframe.

**Register:** simple present or conditional ("will come", "would crush"), concrete violent-precise verbs (lunges, slams, scoops, rips, impaled, crush), second person wherever possible, precise numbers ("18 million gallons", "140 pounds of meat", "a few hundred feet"), vocabulary a 10-year-old follows. Delivery is calm and matter-of-fact while the content is wild or dark - that contrast IS the channel voice.

## Ending taxonomy (one punch sentence, never a summary)

1. **The reframe:** "Meaning what's holding him back isn't the glass, it's the pain of what happened before."
2. **The withheld answer:** the AI video ends "...and sent back its one word answer" - and never reveals it. Maximum open loop, comment-section fuel.
3. **The cycle close:** "...until it's ready to swim away and repeat the cycle." (narrative loop back to line one)
4. **The dark landing:** "After decades of looking, she's still gone. No one knows what happened."
5. **The persona flash:** "...which is why you should just buy an actual glow stick, you weirdo." (personality allowed ONLY in the final beat)

## Titles are claims, not questions

"Catfish Tricks Another Fish Into Adoption" / "The Invisible Barrier" / "The Scariest Safari Rule" / "She Vanished While Her Parents Ate Dinner" / "Housing A Megalodon In An Aquarium". Statement or noun-phrase with a curiosity gap baked in. "How To..." and "Can..." appear occasionally; "Why does..." never appears in the top ten.

## Sensitive-content handling

The true-crime video retells a world-famous missing-child case with no names, no location, no dates - the story shape without the identifying detail. Adopt the same rule: real incidents anonymized, no victim names, no speculation presented as fact.
