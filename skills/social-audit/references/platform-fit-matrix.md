# Platform-Fit Matrix

Use this matrix in Phase 2 Category 6 (Platform-Fit) and Phase 3 per-platform verdicts. Determines when to recommend START, KILL, or DEFEND.

Rule: brands get penalised for NOT being on a high-fit platform, not for being off a low-fit platform.

## Verdict Logic

For each platform, apply this decision tree:

### When to recommend **START** (brand absent, but should be)
- Industry fit ≥ ★★ AND ICP age bracket matches platform AND ≥2 of 3 competitors have presence
- OR industry fit = ★★★ regardless of competitors
- Reason template: "ICP is [demo], platform's dominant audience is [demo match], [X] of 3 competitors post [Y/week]. START."

### When to recommend **DEFEND** (not active, but claim handle)
- Handle is unclaimed or squatted on a ★★ or ★ platform
- Brand has potential expansion into that audience later
- Reason template: "Claim handle defensively to prevent squatting. 10-minute task."

### When to recommend **KILL** (present, shouldn't be)
- Platform fit = — AND effort spent visible (posting, time invested) AND no engagement traction
- OR brand is actively making content for a platform their ICP doesn't use
- Reason template: "Low fit for [industry/ICP]. Redirect time/budget to [higher-fit platform]."

### When to recommend **FIX** (present but underperforming)
- Platform fit ≥ ★★ AND brand has presence AND performance below benchmark
- Reason template: "Fit is strong, execution is weak. Specific fixes below."

### When to recommend **KEEP** (working well)
- Platform fit ≥ ★ AND performance meets or exceeds benchmark AND consistent cadence
- Reason template: "On-strategy and performing. Maintain current approach."

## Special Cases

### B2B startup with only LinkedIn
Don't score them down for missing Pinterest. DO flag if they have zero YouTube (for demos) or no short-form (TikTok/Reels) for founder-led content.

### Local service (plumber, café, hair salon)
Don't score them down for missing LinkedIn or YouTube. DO heavily weight Facebook (local discovery) + Instagram (visual) + Google (via reputation-audit, not here).

### Regulated industry (health, legal, finance)
Note platform-specific advertising limitations but don't penalise presence scoring. Weight LinkedIn + YouTube (educational content safe) higher; de-weight TikTok if compliance makes it impractical.

### Enterprise (10,000+ employees)
Expect presence on 6-8 platforms with dedicated teams. Benchmarks scale up:
- LinkedIn: 50k+ followers minimum
- Instagram: 100k+ if B2C
- YouTube: 10k+ subscribers minimum for brand channels
- Ad library: multiple concurrent campaigns expected

### Solo / SMB (< 10 employees)
Adjust benchmarks down. Focus audit on 2-3 platforms not 8. Recommend concentration not breadth.

### Creator / Personal brand
Expect presence on creator-native platforms (IG, TikTok, YT, X). LinkedIn situational. Score on content volume + engagement depth over reach.

## How to Derive ICP Quickly Without Client Input

1. Homepage hero copy — who is it addressing?
2. Product/service pricing — enterprise vs SMB vs consumer
3. Language + tone — professional vs casual vs Gen Z
4. Testimonials — who are the named customers?
5. Blog content — who are articles written for?
6. LinkedIn "employees" count + titles → company size + vertical
7. Industry + product category → standard ICP assumptions for that category

If ICP is genuinely ambiguous after these checks, flag it as a finding ("Brand positioning unclear — ICP could be X or Y — recommend customer discovery before social investment") and recommend the safer platform set.
