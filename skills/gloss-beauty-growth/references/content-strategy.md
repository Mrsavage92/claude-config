# Gloss Beauty - Content Strategy Reference

_Last updated: 15 May 2026_

---

## The Core Problem

Louise is posting regularly but getting low engagement, few new followers and little to no direct conversion to bookings. The issue is not effort - it is content type and intent.

Most beauty accounts post content that gets likes from other artists or passive scrollers. That does not pay. Content needs to attract the specific people most likely to book.

---

## Who the Content Should Attract

### Makeup / Bridal

- Brides planning a Sunshine Coast wedding (often 6-18 months out)
- Mothers of the bride / bridesmaids looking for a makeup artist
- Local women going to formals, events, races
- Anyone searching locally for event or occasion makeup

### Nails

- Local Sunshine Coast women wanting BIAB/gel nails
- Existing clients who need rebooking
- People who see a result and want the same thing

---

## What Actually Works vs What Wastes Time

### Works

- **Clear before/after or result photos** with a specific caption that names the service, the occasion and the location
- **"I have availability" posts** with a direct CTA and booking link
- **Local-specific content** - mentioning the Sunshine Coast, specific venues, local events
- **Transformation content** - seeing the process or the result drives enquiries more than polished editorial
- **Social proof** - sharing reviews, client testimonials, reposts of client photos
- **Behind the scenes** - real prep, travel to a wedding, getting-ready room setup
- **Specific occasion hooks** - "Wedding season is coming", "Formal season", "Races prep"
- **Nails: fresh set reveals** with colour, style and the Acuity link
- **Nails: rebooking reminders** timed around typical appointment cycles
- **Local Facebook group posts** - Sunshine Coast community, local buy/swap/sell, wedding groups

### Does Not Work (stop doing these)

- Generic beauty tips not tied to a booking outcome
- Reposted trend content with no local relevance
- Posts with no CTA or booking instruction
- Aesthetic-only posts with no information about what was done or who it is for
- Posting without a caption that tells the viewer what to do next
- Posting nails content on the same feed/caption without clearly separating it from makeup

## Caption Formula

A caption that converts should include:

1. What was done (service name, style)
2. Who it was for (occasion - bride, formal, event, nails client)
3. Location signal (Sunshine Coast or specific area)
4. What the viewer should do (enquire, DM, book via link)

**Example - Makeup:**
> Bridal morning on the Sunshine Coast. Soft glam for [first name], ready by 10am for her ceremony at [venue name or area]. [Enquiry CTA]

**Example - Nails:**
> Fresh BIAB set - [colour/style]. Book your next set through the link in bio or DM me your inspo. [Acuity link]

## Local Facebook Group Strategy

For nails and makeup both, posting in local Sunshine Coast Facebook groups is a high-leverage, zero-cost activity.

Post types that work in groups:

- Availability post: "Hi, I'm a local [makeup artist / nail tech] on the Sunshine Coast. I have [X] spots available this week. Here's a few recent results..."
- Before/after with "message me if you'd like to book"
- Seasonal prompt: "School formal season coming up - taking bookings now"

Rules for group posts:

- Always check group rules before posting
- Do not post nails content in makeup-focused groups and vice versa
- Include a result photo where possible - text-only posts perform poorly
- Keep the tone local and personal, not salesy

## When Engagement Is Low - Do This

1. Post an availability/CTA post immediately with a direct offer
2. Post a real result with a specific caption (not aesthetic-only)
3. Post in 1-2 local Facebook groups
4. DM 2-3 recent clients with a rebooking reminder (nails) or check-in (makeup)
5. Ask a recent happy client to leave a review or share their photo

Do not post more of the same thing hoping it performs better. Change the format.

## PR and Brand Outreach

For makeup/bridal, outreach targets could include:

- Local Sunshine Coast wedding venues
- Wedding photographers who may recommend artists to clients
- Wedding planners in the region
- Local bridal shops

Outreach tone: personal, local, no hard sell. A simple introduction and a portfolio link.

Template structure (draft - not confirmed as sent):

```
Hi [Name],

I'm Louise from Gloss Beauty by Louise - a mobile bridal and event makeup artist based on the Sunshine Coast.

I work with brides across the region and I'm always looking to connect with other local wedding professionals. I'd love to be someone you could recommend to clients who are looking for a makeup artist.

I've attached a few recent photos and my website is www.glossbeauty.com.au.

Happy to chat if you'd like to know more.

Louise
```

## Improving a Failing Post

If a post got low engagement or no enquiries, ask:

1. Did the caption have a clear CTA?
2. Did it name the service and location?
3. Was the image strong - was the result obvious?
4. Did it target the right audience (buyer vs. general scroller)?
5. Was it posted in the right place (feed + local group)?

Fix: rewrite the caption with a clear service name, location signal, occasion type and direct CTA. Repost if the image is strong enough.
